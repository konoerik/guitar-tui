## The Lick

The phrase begins in A minor pentatonic position 1 (root at fret 5, low E),
ascends to the top of the position, then crosses into position 2 territory
on the B and high e strings before resolving back down to the root.

The D note on the high e string (fret 10) is the first note that belongs
exclusively to position 2 — it sits above the position 1 ceiling. Reaching
it and coming back is the bridge between the two shapes.

## Looper Setup

Record **Am – G**, two bars each, at 85–95 BPM. The phrase ascends over Am
and resolves back during the G bar. The D note (fret 10) creates mild tension
over Am (it is the 4th — not in the pentatonic, but briefly audible as a
passing tone at speed) and resolves cleanly over G where D is the 5th.

## What to Listen For

The phrase covers nearly two octaves. Notice how the high e string version
of the same run sounds different from the low-string version — same notes,
different register, different character. Experiment with starting the phrase
on the G bar instead of the Am bar and hear how the note relationships shift.

## Technique Notes

The position shift happens between fret 8 (B string) and fret 10 (high e
string) — extend the pinky rather than shifting the hand. The hand stays in
position 1 geometry; only the pinky reaches into position 2 territory. This
is the most efficient way to connect positions without a full shift.
