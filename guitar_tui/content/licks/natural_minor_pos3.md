## The Lick

Position 3 of the A natural minor sits between the 8th and 12th frets, with most of the action around frets 9–12. This phrase ascends a complete octave of the natural minor scale — E to E — using the most characteristic tones of the mode. The result is bright and declarative, with a classical flavour that contrasts with the darker, heavier feel of positions 1 and 2.

**A natural minor: A B C D E F G. This phrase uses E F G A B C D E — one complete ascending octave. The F (b6) in beat 2 and B (major 2nd) in beat 5 are the tones that mark this as natural minor rather than Aeolian pentatonic.**

## Looper Setup

Set up: Am – F – C – G, one bar each.

- **Tempo**: 75–90 BPM
- **Feel**: Smooth, even eighth notes; let the phrase breathe at the top
- **Phrasing**: Start on the E (G string fret 9) on beat 1 of the Am bar. The high E at the end lands on beat 1 of the G bar — the phrase arrives on the 5th degree over the VII chord, which sounds bright and open before the Am repeats.

## Why This Works

Ascending scale phrases work because listeners hear the tension building with each step — natural minor makes that arc particularly expressive because of the half-step between E–F (the 5th and b6) near the bottom and the whole-step skip from D to E at the top. The phrase lands on the 5th degree (high E), which is stable but not completely resolved, encouraging the ear to loop back.

The backing progression (i – VI – III – VII) is one of the most common minor progressions in rock — Aeolian harmony with a strong pull back to the tonic. The phrase works against all four chords.

## Technique Notes

- **Hand position**: anchor the first finger at fret 9. Reach to fret 12 with the fourth finger; there is no need to shift position.
- **String crossing**: every note moves to a higher string. Use a down-stroke on each new string to keep the motion fluid.
- **The top E (e string fret 12)**: hold this note for the full two beats. Adding vibrato here — a slight, wide oscillation — gives the phrase a singing quality and signals the end of the line clearly.
