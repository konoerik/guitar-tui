## The Lick

Melodically this is almost nothing: three notes per bar out of position 1. Rhythmically it is the 3-3-2 pattern — hits on beat 1, the "&" of 2, and beat 4 — and that placement is what makes it a riff instead of an exercise. Bar 2 answers bar 1 and resolves to the root.

**Bar 1: A C D. Bar 2: E C A.** Count all eight eighth-note slots aloud — "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &" — and let each note ring until the next hit. The second hit carries across beat 3 without restriking; that anticipation is the engine of the groove.

## Looper Setup

Record **Am – Am – G – G**, one bar each, at 85–95 BPM. The riff loops over both chords; over G, the D that ends bar 1 becomes the 5th and sits even more solidly.

## What to Listen For

Play the same six notes as straight quarter notes: melody intact, groove gone. Then restore the 3-3-2 and feel the forward lean return. This before/after is the clearest demonstration that rhythm placement — not note choice — is what makes riffs move.

## Technique Notes

- **All downstrokes** at this tempo — the uniform attack suits the pattern's weight.
- **Do not clip the long notes**: each `1` and `&2` hit rings for three eighth-slots. Cutting them short turns the groove into a march.
- **Tap your foot on the quarter note** while the hands play the pattern. If the foot drifts toward the hits, slow down — foot and hands are two independent layers, and keeping them separate is the skill.
