## The Lick

A straight descent from the open high E down to the low register, walking every
scale tone. Flamenco melody is fundamentally *falling* melody; this is the shape
in its purest form.

**E Phrygian dominant = E F G# A B C D. The G# (G string fret 1) is the major
3rd that separates this from plain Phrygian; the F is the half step that makes
it Spanish.**

## Looper Setup

Record **E – F – E – F**, one bar each, with a strong strummed attack at about
110 BPM. Start the descent on the high open E as the loop hits the F chord —
the melody falls as the harmony leans, and both resolve to E together.

## Why This Works

Over the F chord, the descent's C, A, and F are all chord tones — the line
agrees with the bII completely. Over E, the G# and B lock into the major tonic.
The two half-step pairs (F→E at the bottom, C→B in the middle) act as built-in
cadences: everywhere the line pauses, it has just resolved downward by a half
step. That is the *cante* gesture.

## Technique Notes

Play it rest-stroke style if you can — each finger landing on the next string —
for the percussive flamenco attack. Let the final E ring against the open low E
string for the full dominant-that-never-resolves effect.
