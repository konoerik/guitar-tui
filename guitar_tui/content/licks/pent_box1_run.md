## Looper Setup

Record **Am – G – F – E**, one bar each at a comfortable tempo (try 80 BPM).
The phrase resolves on A (the root) which lands squarely over the Am chord.
After running the phrase up, come back down the same notes and listen to how
each note sits against each chord.

## What to Listen For

The G note (B string fret 8) creates slight tension over the Am chord — it is
the b7 in that context. Over the G chord it becomes the root. This is why
pentatonic phrases sound interesting across chord changes: every note has a
different relationship to whichever chord is underneath.

## Technique Notes

Play with alternate picking (down–up–down–up–down). Keep your first finger
anchored at the 5th fret — it does not leave the string between notes on the
same string. Use your ring finger for fret 7 and your pinky for fret 8.
