## The Lick

The B.B. King box centers on the B and high e strings at frets 5–8. Most notes are straight minor pentatonic — but the signature is bending the B string from fret 5 (E, the 5th) up a whole step to F♯ (fret 7 pitch). F♯ is not in A minor pentatonic; bending into it is what gives the phrase its tension and yearning.

The `5b7` on the B string means: fret 5 (note: E), bend up a whole step to reach the pitch of fret 7 (F♯). The `~` indicates vibrato held on the bent note. Then the phrase resolves: G on the B string (fret 8, the b7), then back to A on the high e (fret 5, the root).

## Looper Setup

Record the first four bars of the 12-bar blues in A: **A7 – A7 – D7 – A7** at a slow, heavy tempo (60–70 BPM). Let the lick breathe — play two or three notes, then pause and let the backing fill the silence. The space between notes is as important as the notes themselves. This is call-and-response phrasing: your guitar calls, the backing track answers in the silence.

## What to Listen For

**The bend.** F♯ does not exist as a fretted note in A minor pentatonic position 1 — you create it by bending fret 5 (E) up one whole step. If the bend is shallow, the pitch lands between E and F♯ — expressive but imprecise. Target the exact pitch. Use your ear, not just your fingers.

**Vibrato after the bend.** Hold the bent note at F♯ and add a slow, wide vibrato. This is the B.B. King signature: the note sustains and sings rather than decaying immediately.

**Resolution.** The high e root (A, fret 5) is the resting point. After any phrase in the box, returning to that note over the A7 chord creates immediate resolution.

## Technique Notes

Bend with the ring finger (fret 5), supported by the middle and index fingers behind it — all three fingers pushing the string upward together. A single-finger bend produces weak, unstable pitch. The supported bend gives control over pitch and enough leverage for vibrato without fatigue.

Keep the thumb hooked over the top of the neck for leverage during the bend. This is one of the few techniques where a thumb-over grip is the correct approach.
