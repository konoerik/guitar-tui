## The Lick

The B.B. King box is a four-note cluster at the top of pentatonic position 1:
frets 7–10 on the B string and frets 5–8 on the high e string. Unlike running
scale patterns, this lick stays within that small area and uses bends and
vibrato to extract maximum expression from minimal notes.

The F# on the B string at fret 8 is not in A minor pentatonic — it is a
**whole-step bend from E (fret 7) up to F#**. That bent note is the B.B. King
sound: reaching a note between the scale tones by bending into it.

## Looper Setup

Record the first four bars of the 12-bar blues in A: **A7 – A7 – D7 – A7**
at a slow, heavy tempo (60–70 BPM). Let the lick breathe — play two or three
notes, then pause and let the backing fill the silence. The space between notes
is as important as the notes themselves. This is call-and-response phrasing:
your guitar calls, the backing track answers in the silence.

## What to Listen For

**The bend.** The F# does not exist as a fretted note in this position — you
must create it by bending fret 7 (E) up one whole step. If the bend is shallow,
you get a pitch somewhere between E and F# — expressive but not precise. Target
the exact pitch. Use your ear, not just your fingers.

**Vibrato after the bend.** Hold the bent note at F# and add a slow, wide
vibrato. This is the B.B. King signature: the note sustains and sings rather
than decaying immediately.

**Resolution.** The high e string root (A, fret 5) is the resting point. After
any phrase in the box, returning to that note over the A7 chord creates
immediate resolution.

## Technique Notes

Bend with the ring finger (fret 7), supported by the middle and index fingers
behind it — all three fingers pushing the string upward together. A single-
finger bend produces weak, unstable pitch. The supported bend gives you control
over the pitch and enough leverage for vibrato without fatigue.

Keep the thumb hooked over the top of the neck for leverage during the bend.
This is one of the few techniques where thumb-over grip is the correct approach.
