## The Lick

Position 5 of the A minor pentatonic lives between the 2nd and 5th frets — just below position 1. Most players skip it entirely because it feels like an awkward in-between. This phrase shows its value: it connects naturally into position 1 via the low E string, making it the bridge between the top and bottom of the scale across the neck.

**A minor pentatonic: A C D E G. The phrase moves through position 5 (G–C D–E G–A) and steps onto position 1 (A–C on the low E string).**

## Looper Setup

Set up: Am for two bars, G for two bars, repeat.

- **Tempo**: 80–100 BPM
- **Feel**: Flowing eighth notes; the position shift should feel seamless
- **Phrasing**: Start on beat 1. The drop to the low E (fret 5) lands on beat 7 of the 8-beat phrase, giving it a natural fall into the repeat.

## Why This Works

The G string and B string in position 5 run through the root (A at G string fret 2 and e string fret 5) before dropping to the low E root at fret 5 — the same fret that opens position 1. That shared root note is the handshake between the two positions: the moment your first finger lands on low E fret 5, you are already in position 1.

Playing this phrase teaches you to use position 5 as a launching pad rather than a destination. Practice it until the shift feels like one continuous motion.

## Technique Notes

- **Hand position**: anchor the first finger at fret 2 for the G and B string notes, then shift forward to fret 5 on the e string, then drop to the low E string without repositioning.
- **The shift**: the move from e string (fret 5) to low E string (fret 5) is the same fret but a large physical jump — keep your wrist relaxed and let the pick follow the angle of the strings.
- **Listen for**: the low A on the final two beats should sound darker and heavier than the high A two beats earlier — that contrast is the point.
