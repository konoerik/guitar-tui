## The Lick

Descend from C (high e, fret 8) through the natural minor scale: C – A – G –
E – D – C. The phrase covers three strings and crosses two octaves of the scale.

## Looper Setup

Record **Am – F – C – G**, two bars each. Start the phrase at the beginning of
the Am bar. The phrase lands on the low C (G string, fret 5) which sits over
the C chord as a root note — a satisfying harmonic resolution at the end of the
descent. Loop and repeat.

## Why This Works

Natural minor has two notes the pentatonic does not: the 2nd (B) and the 6th
(F). In this descending phrase, neither appears — but having the full scale
available means you can extend or vary the phrase by adding those notes.
The D (4th degree) and G (b7) give the descent a slightly more "classical" or
"cinematic" quality than a pure pentatonic run: the stepwise motion (no gaps)
feels more melodic and less riff-like.

## Technique Notes

Play legato if possible — hammer-ons descending (actually pull-offs for descent)
or simply pick each note cleanly and let the phrase breathe. Try the phrase at
50 BPM and focus on even timing between all six notes before increasing speed.
The phrase can be extended upward by starting at e fret 12 (A, an octave
higher) and descending further.
