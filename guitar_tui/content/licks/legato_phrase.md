## The Lick

Two picks, five notes. Pick the B string, hammer to the top of the string pair, cross to the high e string, hammer again, then pull off back down. The phrase flows in one continuous motion — no pick-click interrupting the line.

All five notes are in A minor pentatonic position 1. The hammers move up; the pull-off snaps back. Practice the pull-off last — it requires both fingers to be in position simultaneously before you snap.

## Looper Setup

Record a static **Am** chord at a medium tempo (80–100 BPM) and loop it. The legato quality of this phrase sits best against a clean, sustaining chord rather than a busy rhythm part. Once comfortable, move to a full 12-bar Am blues.

## Technique Notes

The hammer-on and pull-off must match the volume of the two picked notes. Listen for any drop in level on the hammered or pulled notes — if they are quieter, slow down and apply more force. Volume consistency across all five notes is the goal.

Keep the lower finger (fret 5) planted on the string throughout the pull-off. It must be there before the snap, not after.
