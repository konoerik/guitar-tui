## The Lick

One idea, three times. Bar 1 states a three-note motif (A–C–D). Bar 2 replants the same shape two scale steps higher (D–E–G). Bar 3 grows it one note longer and brings it home. The listener hears *one thought developing*, not three separate licks.

**Bar 1: A C D (the seed). Bar 2: D E G (same contour, higher ground). Bar 3: G A E D → A (extended and resolved).** Each bar starts where the previous one ended — D links bars 1–2, G links bars 2–3.

## Looper Setup

Record **Am – F – G – Am**, one bar each, 75–90 BPM. Each statement of the motif lands on a new chord, which recolors the same shape — the D that ends bar 1 is the 5th of G by the time bar 3 resolves.

## What to Listen For

The power of the lick is recognition: by bar 2 the listener knows the shape and hears it *as* a repetition-with-change. That is what makes development feel intentional. Notice the linking notes — each phrase begins on the pitch the previous one reached, so the three bars chain into a single line.

## Technique Notes

- **Identical rhythm in bars 1 and 2**: the development is pitch-only; keep the rhythm a clone so the transposition is unmistakable.
- **The held notes**: bars 1 and 2 each end with a full-beat hold plus a rest. Do not trim them — the shape needs its frame.
- **Make your own**: swap in any three-note seed from position 1 and run the same plan (state, transpose up, extend home). This lick is a template, not just a line.
