## The Lick

Question and answer, in opposite colors. Bar one rises through major
pentatonic to a held E; bar two falls through minor pentatonic to the same E.

**Bar one: A – B – C# – E from A major pentatonic (frets 2–5). Bar two:
C – A – G – E from A minor pentatonic (frets 5–8). The C# and the C natural —
a half step apart — are what flip the mood.**

## Looper Setup

Record the first four bars of a **12-bar blues in A** (all A7 works too) at
100 BPM with a shuffle feel. Play the pair over bars one and two, rest over
bars three and four. Once comfortable, move the pair around the full 12-bar
form and notice where each color sits best.

## Why This Works

Over A7, both pentatonics are legal and they argue politely: the C# agrees
with the chord's major 3rd (sweet), while the C natural rubs against it (the
blues sound itself). Pairing them as statement and answer gives a two-bar
exchange with a complete emotional arc — bright question, gritty reply,
resolved on the shared E. The switch happens at the phrase boundary, exactly
as the lesson prescribes: finish the thought, breathe, change color.

## Technique Notes

The hand relocates between bars — frets 2–4 to frets 5–8. Use the held E at
the end of bar one to make the shift: while it rings, move the hand up so the
fourth finger is ready over the high C at fret 8. The rest between phrases is
not dead time; it is when the color change registers with the listener.
