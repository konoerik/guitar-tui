## The Lick

This phrase sits in the second position of the G major scale (5th–8th fret area) and ascends through the high strings with a clear, vocal quality. It targets the major 6th (E) and major 7th (F#) — the two notes that define the full major scale sound — before resolving to the root. The result feels bright, optimistic, and finished.

## Looper Setup

Set up: G – D – Em – C, one bar each.

- **Tempo**: 75–95 BPM
- **Feel**: Light, singing eighth notes; imagine a vocalist delivering this line
- **Phrasing**: Start on the A (high e, fret 5) on beat 3 of the D bar (pickup feel). The D on G string (fret 7) lands on beat 1 of the C bar, creating a slightly unresolved suspension that resolves when the phrase repeats over G.

## Why This Works

The phrase ascends in two waves: first through the high e and B strings (A–B–E–F#–G), then continuing on the G string (B–C–D). Each wave ends a step higher than where it started. The F#–G half-step in the first wave gives an immediate major-key signal; the B–C–D continuation extends the phrase naturally across the next three scale degrees.

The ending on D (G string fret 7) leaves the phrase harmonically open — the 5th of G, which wants to resolve back to the root. This makes the looper repeat feel natural and satisfying.

## Technique Notes

- **Hand position**: anchor the first finger at fret 4 for the G string notes; shift slightly to fret 5 for the e and B string passages.
- **The F# (B string fret 7)**: pause mentally on this note before stepping to G. The half-step resolution is the emotional peak of the phrase.
- **Singing it**: before playing, sing or hum the phrase. Major scale lines are among the most singable in music — if you can hear it, the notes will feel more natural under the fingers.
