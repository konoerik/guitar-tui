## The Lick

Position 3 of the A minor pentatonic sits between the 9th and 13th frets. Most players never reach it because positions 1 and 2 feel complete — this phrase shows what you gain by going higher. The phrase climbs to the high e string, then descends back to the root on the B string, creating a satisfying arc.

## Looper Setup

Set up: Am for two bars, G for two bars, repeat.

- **Tempo**: 80–95 BPM
- **Feel**: Straight eighth notes; dark and deliberate
- **Phrasing**: Start on beat 1 of the Am bar. The final A (B string, fret 10) falls on beat 1 of the repeat.

## Why This Works

The phrase outlines the full span of position 3 without any position-shift — every note is reachable without moving the hand. It lands on the root (A) twice: once on the way up (B string 10) and once at the end. That double resolution makes the phrase feel complete and loopable.

The jump from G string (fret 12) to B string (fret 10) in the middle is the key interval — a minor 3rd that gives the phrase its characteristic drop.

## Technique Notes

- **Hand position**: anchor the first finger at fret 10. The only stretch is the 3-fret reach on the B and G strings (frets 9 and 13).
- **Picking**: alternate pick throughout; the phrase flows naturally with down–up pairs.
- **Tone**: let the final A ring for the full two beats before looping.
