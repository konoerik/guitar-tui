The capstone lesson's skeleton line: one bar per chord, two chord tones per
bar, connected as smoothly as position 1 allows.

**Bar by bar: Am gets C and E; C gets G and E; D gets F# (B string, fret 7);
F gets F natural (fret 6) — the two sixths a bar apart on the same string;
E gets its G#; and the final Am receives the resolution.**

## Looper Setup

Record **Am – C – D – F – Am – E – Am**, one bar per chord at 80 BPM (give
the final Am two bars so the loop breathes before repeating). These are the
traditional changes analyzed in the Songbook's House of the Rising Sun entry
— the same loop serves both.

## Why This Works

No single scale fits this progression, which is precisely what makes the line
instructive: the melody changes its mind exactly when the harmony does. The
F#-then-F pair across the D and F bars is "the chords vote" made audible, and
the G#-to-A ending is the harmonic minor cadence in one gesture. Half notes
sound skeletal on purpose — this is the load-bearing structure a real solo
decorates.

## Technique Notes

Play it as written until the fret 7 / fret 6 / fret 5 walk down the B string
feels inevitable, then start decorating: approach each target from a scale
step below, add a hammer-on into the F#, vibrato on the G#. Keep the targets
on the downbeats no matter how much decoration accumulates — lose the
downbeats and the line stops narrating the chords.
