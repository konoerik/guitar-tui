## The Lick

On the A string: descend from E (fret 7) through Eb (fret 6, the blue note)
to D (fret 5). The Eb is a chromatic passing tone — it lasts one eighth note
and is never a resting pitch. Above it, a high D on the G string anchors the phrase.

**A string fret 7 = E (5th). Fret 6 = Eb (b5, the blue note). Fret 5 = D (root of D7).**

## Looper Setup

Record **A7 – D7**, two bars each. Time the lick so the descent lands on D
(fret 5) just as the D7 chord arrives. The phrase acts as a connecting line
between the two chords — the E belongs to A7, the D belongs to D7, and the
Eb is the tension between them.

## Why This Works

The Eb (b5 relative to A, the tritone) is simultaneously dissonant and
gravitational. It appears in neither the A7 nor the D7 chord, which is exactly
why it creates movement: it can only be a passing tone, pulling toward a target.
The target is D — the root of the IV chord, which arrives on the next bar.

## Technique Notes

Keep the tempo slow enough that the Eb is audible as a distinct pitch, not a
smear. Use a slight vibrato on the opening D (G string, fret 7) to add
expression before descending. This lick is the skeleton of countless BB King
and Albert King phrases.
