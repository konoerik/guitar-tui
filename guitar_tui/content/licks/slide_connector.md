## The Lick

A two-string phrase that starts on the D string (low end of position 1), moves to the B string, and climbs to the root via a slide. The slide replaces the mechanical position shift — the phrase arrives without a seam.

D string fret 5 = G (b7); fret 7 = A (root). B string fret 5 = E (5th); fret 8 = G (b7); fret 10 = A (root). Every note is in A minor pentatonic. The slide from fret 8 to fret 10 on the B string — G to A — is the phrase's arc, landing on the root with vibrato.

## Looper Setup

Record **Am** for two or four bars at a relaxed tempo (70–90 BPM). Focus on the slide: commit to the destination (fret 10) and do not release string pressure mid-glide. The vibrato at the top should be slow and wide — this is the phrase's resting point, not a tense moment.

## Technique Notes

The slide uses the ring finger on the B string throughout. Keep it pressing firmly as it moves — the moment you ease pressure, the glide cuts out.

Set the vibrato after the slide has fully arrived at fret 10. Oscillating while the finger is still moving blurs the pitch; the vibrato should start when the note has stabilized.
