## The Lick

Bend the B string at fret 8 (note: G) up a whole step to the pitch of fret 10
(note: A). Hold the bend for one beat, release slowly back to G, then step down
to D on the G string.

**Fret 8 on the B string = G. Bend up a whole step (shown as `8b10`) to reach A — the root of A7.**

## Looper Setup

Record **A7** for four bars — just the one chord. The bend phrase is most
powerful when heard against a static dominant 7th chord. Once comfortable,
move to the full 12-bar form: A7 (4 bars) → D7 (2 bars) → A7 (2 bars) →
E7 – D7 – A7 – E7.

## Why This Works

The bent note reaches A, which is the root of the A7 chord — maximum
consonance. But the approach from G (the b7) carries tension through the
attack, so the resolution feels earned. This is the core of blues phrasing:
set up tension, resolve it deliberately.

## Technique Notes

Push the string *toward* the floor (downward bend on the B string). Use all
three fingers behind the bending finger for support — do not bend with one
finger alone. The release should be controlled, not a snap. Vibrato on the
held pitch is optional but adds expression.
