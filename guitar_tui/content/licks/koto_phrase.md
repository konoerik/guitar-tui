## The Lick

An arch: up from the root to the b6, then a mirrored descent home. Nine slow
notes. The spaces between them matter as much as the notes.

**A hirajoshi = A B C E F. The peak of the arc is F (b6), which immediately
falls back to E — the scale's signature sigh. The descent ends C → B → A, the
stepwise fall to the root.**

## Looper Setup

Record a single low **Am** chord — or just the open A string — and let it ring
for four slow bars at 70 BPM. This music historically sits over a drone, not a
progression. Play the phrase once per loop and *wait* out the remaining space.

## Why This Works

Both of hirajoshi's half steps are used as downward resolutions: F releases to
E at the arc's peak, and C–B–A closes the phrase. Nothing pushes upward — no
bends, no upward slurs. The identical notes on the way down mirror the way up,
which gives the phrase its formal, composed quality: an arch, not a riff.

## Technique Notes

Let every note ring into the next (koto strings are never damped mid-phrase).
Attack near the bridge for a plucked, harp-like tone. The single most important
note is the F at the top: give it a full beat, no vibrato, and let its fall to
E be heard as a release rather than a passing tone.
