## The Lick

A short rising statement to the high root, a full two-beat silence, then a quiet two-note echo that settles back onto the same root. The silence is not a gap between two licks — it is the middle of one.

**Statement: E G A (rising to the root, held). Echo: C A (falling back onto the same root, held long).** The whole second bar is two notes and sustain.

## Looper Setup

Record a bar or two of **Am** at 70–80 BPM and let it cycle. Start the statement on beat 1. The echo starts on beat 1 of the next bar — everything between is deliberate, counted silence.

## What to Listen For

Play it twice: once as written, once with the rest filled with extra notes. The filled version says less. The held final note over the looping Am is the payoff — a phrase that ends before the bar does projects confidence, and the backing track carries the time for you.

## Technique Notes

- **End the statement cleanly**: after the held A on the high e (fret 5), lift the fretting finger slightly (or touch the string with a spare picking-hand finger) so the silence starts *on purpose* rather than by decay.
- **Count the rest** — "2 &, 3 &, 4 &" — and breathe out through it. If you feel the itch to play, that is the exercise working.
- **The echo is quieter**: pick the C–A softer than the statement. Dynamics are part of phrasing, and an echo that whispers makes the silence before it feel intentional.
