## The Lick

The Andalusian cadence — Em – Dm – C – B — is the harmonic engine of flamenco and much of the darker end of rock and metal. This phrase descends through E Phrygian in open position, one string set at a time, covering the characteristic F natural (b2) on the first string and resolving to a low G (b3) that sits perfectly against the C and B chords at the end of the progression.

**E Phrygian: E F G A B C D. This phrase uses G F E D C B A G — descending b3 → b2 → root → b7 → b6 → 5 → 4 → b3. The F natural (b2, beat 2) is the defining Phrygian sound. Every other mode has a major 2nd; only Phrygian has a half step between the root and the second degree.**

## Looper Setup

Set up: Em – Dm – C – B, two bars each.

- **Tempo**: 75–90 BPM
- **Feel**: Deliberate, even eighth notes — the character comes from the notes, not speed
- **Phrasing**: Start the phrase on the Em bar. The final G (G string open) lands on beat 1 of the B bar — the b3 against the major V chord creates a brief clash before the Em repeats. That friction is intentional; it is the sound of Phrygian in resolution.

## Why This Works

The Andalusian cadence descends from the tonic through the bVII and bVI to the V — a stepwise root motion that is immediately recognizable as flamenco/metal Phrygian. This phrase mirrors that descent melodically: it starts on G (b3, root of the Dm chord), steps through F (b2, the Phrygian fingerprint), resolves to E (root), then descends through the rest of the scale.

The open strings are an asset here, not a limitation. In flamenco guitar, open strings ring against fretted notes to create a harmonic shimmer. Let all notes sustain slightly rather than muting between beats — the overlapping resonance is part of the sound.

## Technique Notes

- **Right hand**: use alternating down-up picking, or classical-style rest strokes (apoyando) — striking through the string to rest on the next string — for a more authoritative, classical tone.
- **The F (high e fret 1)**: play this with the index finger. The half-step between fret 1 (F) and open (E) is the center of the phrase — let the transition to the open E ring cleanly.
- **String crossing**: every three beats the phrase moves to the next lower string pair. The string cross after fret 0 on each string (open string to next) is the coordination point to practice slowly.
- **Final note (G string open)**: let this ring for the full two beats. Muting with the right hand palm after two beats gives the phrase a clean ending before the loop repeats.
