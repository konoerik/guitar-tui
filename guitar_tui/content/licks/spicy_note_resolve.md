## The Lick

The b6-to-5 resolution is the emotional center of natural minor. This phrase
climbs to the F (B string, fret 6), leans on it, releases to E, and walks home.

**A natural minor = A B C D E F G. The F (B string, fret 6) is the b6 — the
one note in this phrase that could not come from the pentatonic box.**

## Looper Setup

Record a plain **Am** vamp, four bars at 80 BPM — slow enough that the held F
has real weight. Start the phrase on beat one and let the final A ring through
the rest of the loop.

## Why This Works

Everything before the F is pentatonic scaffolding. The F itself sits a half
step above the chord's 5th, and that half step is pure gravity — the ear
hears it *leaning* onto the E before it moves. Granting it two full beats,
then resolving, is the color-versus-tension rule from the lessons performed
in miniature: the tension is featured, not hidden, and then it pays off.

## Technique Notes

Use the second finger on the F so the first finger is already waiting over
fret 5 for the resolution — the E should arrive with no shift and no gap. Add
a slow vibrato to the held F if you have it; the note rewards drama. Do not
rush beats four and five: the resolution is the point of the phrase, and it
needs air on both sides.
