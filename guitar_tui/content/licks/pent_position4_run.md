## Looper Setup

Set up: Am for two bars, G for two bars, repeat.

- **Tempo**: 75–90 BPM
- **Feel**: Driving eighth notes; build energy into the final G
- **Phrasing**: Start on beat 1 of the Am bar. Hold the high G across the bar line into G chord — the dissonance resolves naturally.

## Why This Works

Starting on the root (A at fret 12) anchors the phrase harmonically before it takes off. The ascent crosses four strings cleanly, hitting the minor 7th (G) and root (A) again in the middle, then landing on the high G. That repeated landing on G (minor 7th) gives the phrase its slightly unresolved, driving quality — it wants to loop.

## Technique Notes

- **Hand position**: anchor the first finger at fret 12. The only stretch is the 3-fret gap on the A string (frets 12 to 15).
- **String crossing**: each string change is ascending, so the pick naturally follows the motion downward across the strings.
- **Expression**: add a slight palm mute on the first two notes (A string) to contrast with the open tone of the higher strings.
