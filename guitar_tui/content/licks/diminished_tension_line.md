## The Lick

A rising line built from small cells, each starting a step higher — the
sequence-friendly shape that symmetric scales are made for.

**C diminished (W–H) = C D Eb F Gb Ab A B. The line walks C–D, F–Gb, then
A–B–C: whole step, half step, and the final half step lands the octave.**

## Looper Setup

Record **C°** — one crisp strum per bar, four bars, around 90 BPM. The
diminished chord supplies wall-to-wall tension; the line's job is to organize
it. Time the final B → C so it lands on the loop's downbeat.

## Why This Works

Every other note of this scale is a chord tone of C° (C–Eb–Gb–A), so the line
constantly touches the chord while the in-between notes (D, F, B) push it
forward. The last three notes — A, B, C — tighten the step size as they
approach the root: the scale's built-in acceleration toward resolution.

## Technique Notes

Keep the cells rhythmically identical — the effect comes from the pattern, not
from rubato. Once comfortable, move the entire phrase up 3 frets and play it
again: same scale, same fingering, new register. Chaining the phrase up the
neck in minor-3rd jumps is the classic diminished run.
