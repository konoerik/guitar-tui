## The Lick

Ascending through G major pentatonic from the low G root (fret 3, low E string)
up through the open strings to the B on the A string, then up to D.

**G major pentatonic: G – A – B – D – E (no C, no F#).**

## Looper Setup

Record **G – D – Em – C**, one or two bars each. Let the walk repeat on a loop.
Because every note in G major pentatonic is consonant against every chord in
this progression, you can play the phrase starting on any beat and it will
sound musical.

## Why This Works

Major pentatonic contains no half-step intervals — there is no tension built
in. Every note is a comfortable resting point. This gives major pentatonic
phrases their characteristic brightness and resolution. Compare the same
ascending walk in A minor pentatonic over Am–G–F–E: the notes feel darker
and more ambiguous.

## Technique Notes

This phrase crosses strings in a diagonal pattern — each note is on a different
string. Use strict alternate picking and keep each note ringing for its full
duration before moving to the next string. The phrase can be extended by
continuing up the D, G, B, and e strings in the same major pentatonic pattern.
