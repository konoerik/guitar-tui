## The Lick

A short, repeating phrase centered on D minor with a B natural — the Dorian
signature note. The phrase moves between the A and G strings and stays in the
lower-middle register for a warm, vocal quality.

**D Dorian = D E F G A B C. The B natural (G string fret 4) is the major 6th — the note that would be Bb in D natural minor.**

## Looper Setup

Record **Dm – G**, two bars each, at a relaxed 90 BPM groove tempo. This is the
*Oye Como Va* vamp. Let the phrase repeat — it is designed as a loop, not a
one-time run. The D root at the end leads naturally back to the D at the start.

Over the Dm chord: D, E, G, A, B all sit consonantly.
Over the G chord: B is the major 3rd of G, D is the 5th — the phrase locks in.

## Why This Works

The Dm chord tells your ear "minor." But the G chord (major IV, not minor iv)
introduces the B natural — a note that does not exist in D natural minor. That
single note lifts the progression: it sounds minor but not dark, groovy but not
heavy. This is the Dorian character. If you played Bbm instead of G, the B
natural would disappear and the phrase would become straightforward natural minor.

## Technique Notes

This phrase works best as a rhythm-melody hybrid — let each note ring for its
full duration rather than articulating quickly. Try adding a slight swing
feel (eighth notes uneven, long-short) to match the funk/soul context.
