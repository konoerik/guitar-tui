## Looper Setup

Set up: Am – G – Am – E, one bar each.

- **Tempo**: 70–85 BPM
- **Feel**: Even eighth notes; push slightly into the F (beat 6) to emphasize the half-step arrival
- **Phrasing**: Start on the low G (b7, low E string fret 3) on beat 1 of the Am bar. The phrase resolves to A (root, G string fret 2) on beat 1 of the E bar — arriving on the root over the V chord, creating a clear point of arrival before the Am repeats.

## Why This Works

Starting on the b7 (G, the note just below the root) gives the phrase a sense of gathering momentum before the climb. The E→F half-step on the D string is the expressive peak — a small move with high emotional content. That half-step is what distinguishes natural minor from the minor pentatonic, and landing it clearly in the middle of an ascending phrase trains the ear to hear it as a feature rather than a passing note.

The final note — root A on the G string fret 2 — lands one string above the D string b6 sequence. The ear resolves up through the tension of the F and settles on the root, completing the phrase with a quiet sense of arrival.

## Technique Notes

- **Hand position**: center the hand around frets 3–4 with the index finger reaching back to fret 2 where needed. No position shift is required.
- **Low E to A string crossing (beats 1–3)**: beats 1 and 2 are on the low E string; beats 3 and 4 jump to the A string. Use alternate picking — the string crossing is the main coordination challenge in the first half of the phrase.
- **The E–F half-step (D string, frets 2–3)**: play fret 2 with the index finger, then fret 3 with the middle. Keep the motion deliberate — this is the moment of the phrase. A slight accent on the F (middle finger, fret 3) makes the character of the position audible.
- **Final note**: hold the root A (G string fret 2) for the full two beats. This position is the gateway back to Position 1 — letting the root breathe here reinforces that sense of return before the cycle repeats.
