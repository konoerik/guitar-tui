## The Lick

Seven notes that hit both of the scale's three-fret leaps in a single ascent:
b3 → #4 in the low half, b6 → 7 at the top.

**A Hungarian minor = A B C D# E F G#. The D# (A string fret 6) is the raised
4th; together with the G# it gives the scale its two augmented-2nd leaps.**

## Looper Setup

Record **Am – E**, two bars each, at a stately 90 BPM — this tradition is
dramatic, not fast (at first). Start the phrase on the Am bar; the F–G#–A top
arrives over the E chord and resolves as the loop restarts.

## Why This Works

The line is a chain of leap-and-resolve pairs: C leaps to D#, which resolves
up to E (the 5th); F leaps to G#, which resolves up to A (the root). Each
augmented 2nd is immediately released by a half step — tension bought, then
spent. Romani violin phrasing is built from exactly this currency.

## Technique Notes

Both leaps cross strings (fret 8 to fret 6 on the next string), so the fretting
hand shifts twice. Practice each leap-resolve cell alone — C–D#–E, then F–G#–A —
before chaining them. Once solid, add a slide into the D# for the authentic
vocal swoop.
