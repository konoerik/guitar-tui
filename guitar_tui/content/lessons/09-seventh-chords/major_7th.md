## What Is a Major 7th Chord?

A major 7th chord (written Xmaj7) takes a major chord and adds the *major* 7th — the note that is one half-step below the octave. Formula: **1 – 3 – 5 – 7**.

Unlike the dominant 7th, which adds a *minor* 7th (b7) and creates tension, the major 7th adds a *major* 7th and creates a lush, floating quality. There is no tritone; the chord is consonant and can sit comfortably without resolving. In jazz and pop, Imaj7 and IVmaj7 are the defining sound of a sophisticated harmonic palette.

The distinction to memorise: **X7 = dominant (b7, tense) / Xmaj7 = major 7th (natural 7, dreamy)**.

## The Shape Relationship

Compare each major 7th to its dominant 7th and plain major versions:

- **Emaj7 vs E7 vs E**: E7 has an open G string (G = b7). Emaj7 frets the G string at 1 (G# = major 7th of E). Only one note changes between E7 and Emaj7.
- **Amaj7 vs A7 vs A**: A7 opens the G string (G = b7). Amaj7 frets G string at fret 1 (G# = major 7th of A) — same pattern as E.
- **Dmaj7 vs D**: D frets the B string at fret 3 (D = root). Dmaj7 moves it to fret 2 (C# = major 7th of D). The high e stays at fret 2 (E = 9th, also present in D). Result: three strings at fret 2.
- **Gmaj7 vs G**: G major uses the pinky on the high e at fret 3 (G = root). Gmaj7 moves it to fret 2 (F# = major 7th). One finger drop, one fret lower.

In each case, the major 7th chord simply adjusts or adds the note one half-step below the octave root.

## Sound and Context

The major 7th interval (root to 7th) is 11 semitones — a half-step below the octave. It creates a gentle tension that feels yearning rather than urgent. Common uses:

- **Imaj7**: The I chord with maj7 is a hallmark of jazz, soul, and neo-soul. It is "home but with longing."
- **IVmaj7**: The IV chord with maj7 is smoother than IV alone; it is common in pop and R&B for a laid-back feel.
- **Imaj7 → IVmaj7**: Moving between these two creates the unmistakable sound of jazz ballads and slow soul grooves.

## Major 7th in Common Keys

| Key | Imaj7 | IVmaj7 |
|-----|-------|--------|
| E | Emaj7 | Amaj7 |
| A | Amaj7 | Dmaj7 |
| D | Dmaj7 | Gmaj7 |
| G | Gmaj7 | Cmaj7 |

The open voicings in this lesson give you Imaj7 → IVmaj7 in the keys of E, A, D, and G — the four most guitar-friendly keys — all in open position.
