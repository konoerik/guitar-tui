## The ii7–V7–Imaj7 Progression

The single most important 7th chord progression is **ii m7 → V7 → Imaj7**. It is the backbone of jazz harmony and appears constantly in pop, R&B, and soul. In the key of G:

- **ii m7** = Am7 (A–C–E–G)
- **V7** = D7 (D–F#–A–C)
- **Imaj7** = Gmaj7 (G–B–D–F#)

Each chord shares notes with the next, creating smooth voice-leading. The Am7 → D7 transition shares A and C. The D7 → Gmaj7 resolution delivers the strongest harmonic pull in tonal music — the F# in D7 rises to G (the root of Gmaj7) and the C falls to B (the 3rd of Gmaj7).

## The Imaj7–vi m7–ii m7–V7 Loop

Extending ii7–V7–I to four chords produces the most ubiquitous jazz-pop loop — the circle-of-fifths rotation in a single key:

**Gmaj7 → Em7 → Am7 → D7** (repeat)

Each root moves down a third or up a fifth:
- G → E: down a minor 3rd (or up a major 6th)
- E → A: up a perfect 4th
- A → D: up a perfect 4th (the V7 motion)
- D → G: resolves up a perfect 4th (the V→I cadence)

This loop underlies thousands of songs. Playing it on guitar with the four chords above (all in open position) is the clearest entry point into jazz harmony.

## The I7–IV7 Blues Move

In blues, the dominant 7th is used on the I chord — which creates restlessness even at home. Alternating between I7 and IV7 is the most basic blues move:

| Bar | Chord |
|-----|-------|
| 1–4 | A7 (I7) |
| 5–6 | D7 (IV7) |
| 7–8 | A7 (I7) |
| 9   | E7 (V7) |
| 10  | D7 (IV7) |
| 11–12 | A7 (I7) → E7 turnaround |

All three chords are dominant 7ths. The characteristic "blues sound" comes partly from the harmonic implication: treating every chord as if it needs to resolve somewhere, even when it is the home chord.

## Choosing the Right 7th Quality

| Chord function | Preferred 7th type | Why |
|---------------|-------------------|-----|
| I (tonic) | maj7 | Stable but lush; no tension |
| ii | m7 | Diatonic; smooth lead-in to V |
| V | dominant 7th | Tritone tension; demands I |
| vi | m7 | Diatonic; gentle minor colour |
| Blues I, IV, V | dominant 7th | Gritty; creates forward momentum on every chord |

Understanding these choices lets you match the harmonic vocabulary to the style — maj7 and m7 for jazz-influenced smoothness, dominant 7ths throughout for blues.
