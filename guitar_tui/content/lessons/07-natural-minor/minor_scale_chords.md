## Stacking Thirds in A Minor

Every degree of the natural minor scale generates a chord by stacking thirds using only the notes of the scale. The resulting seven chords are the *diatonic chords* of A minor. Knowing them reveals which chords naturally belong together in a minor key — and why certain progressions sound so inevitable.

The diatonic chords of A natural minor:

| Degree | Numeral | Chord | Type |
|--------|---------|-------|------|
| 1 — A | **i** | Am | minor |
| 2 — B | **ii°** | Bdim | diminished |
| 3 — C | **III** | C | major |
| 4 — D | **iv** | Dm | minor |
| 5 — E | **v** | Em | minor |
| 6 — F | **VI** | F | major |
| 7 — G | **VII** | G | major |

The pattern — minor, diminished, major, minor, minor, major, major — is fixed for any natural minor key.

## The Most Important Chords

**i (Am)** is the tonic — home. Phrases and progressions resolve here.

**VII (G) and VI (F)** are the two major chords built from the upper degrees of the scale. The VII–i movement (G→Am) is the characteristic cadence of natural minor, producing a different resolution than the V–i (Em→Am) — softer, less conclusive, more modal.

**iv (Dm)** is the subdominant minor. The iv–i progression (Dm→Am) is one of the most emotionally direct movements in minor key music.

**v (Em)** — note this is a *minor* 5th chord, not a major V. This is the defining feature that separates natural minor from harmonic minor (which raises the 7th to create a major V chord for a stronger resolution). Natural minor's minor v creates a more modal, less "classical" feel.

## Common Progressions in A Minor

These all use only diatonic chords:

| Progression | Numerals | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Am – F – C – G | i – VI – III – VII | Pop/rock staple |
| Am – G – F – G | i – VII – VI – VII | Classic rock loop |
| Am – Dm – G – Am | i – iv – VII – i | Folk minor cadence |
| Am – F – G – Am | i – VI – VII – i | Modal minor loop |

## Key Transposition

In any minor key, the chord pattern is identical — only the names change. In D minor: i=Dm, II°=E°, III=F, iv=Gm, v=Am, VI=Bb, VII=C. The relationships and the progressions remain the same.
