## Position 4 — A-Shape (frets 12–15)

Position 4 occupies the upper register of the neck, frets 12–15. The root sits on the A string at fret 12 — the octave position — and again on the G string at fret 14. This is the A-shape position, equivalent in structure to the A-shape barre chord region.

## The b6 Appears Early

In this position, the b6 (F) appears as the second note on the low E string (fret 13) and the second note on the high e string (fret 13). Its placement directly adjacent to the 5th (fret 12) on both outer strings means that descending runs through Position 4 encounter the b6 immediately — giving this position a particularly "classical minor" character right from the start of a descending phrase.

## Symmetry Across the Neck

Notice that strings 6 and 1 (both E strings) have identical fret patterns in this position: 12, 13, 15. This symmetry is a useful memory aid — what you play on the low E is mirrored on the high e.

## Fingering

The fret span here (12–15) is four frets. Use strict one-finger-per-fret:
- Fret 12: index
- Fret 13: middle
- Fret 14: ring
- Fret 15: pinky

String 4 (D) has only two notes (frets 12, 14 — index and ring, skipping middle). String 3 (G) also has two notes (frets 12, 14). The B string starts at fret 13 rather than 12 — the position begins one fret higher on that string than on the others, which is the expected behaviour at the B string boundary.
