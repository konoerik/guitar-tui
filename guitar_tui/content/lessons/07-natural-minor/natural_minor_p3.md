## Position 3 — C-Shape (frets 9–13)

Position 3 spans frets 9 to 13 and has its root on the A string (fret 12) and the B string (fret 10). The wider fret span — four frets plus the 13th — makes this one of the more technically demanding positions, though the notes at fret 13 (b3 on string 2) are the only ones that reach beyond the standard four-fret window.

## The 12th Fret Landmark

Fret 12 is the octave — every note at fret 12 is the same pitch class as the open string, one octave higher. The root A appears at fret 12 on the A string (string 5), which is one of the easiest roots to find on the entire neck. Position 3 is built around this octave landmark, making it a practical position for solos in the upper-middle neck region.

## The b3 Extension at Fret 13

The b3 (C) at fret 13 on the B string is the only note outside the four-fret window. Reach it with the pinky while keeping the hand centred around frets 9–12. Avoid shifting the entire hand position — the stretch should come from the pinky alone.

## Fingering

Strings 6 and 5 are handled with just two notes each (two frets apart). Strings 4, 3, and 2 have three notes, requiring index–middle–pinky (or index–ring–pinky, depending on fret spacing). For string 2 (frets 10, 12, 13): index at 10, ring at 12, pinky at 13 — a stretch that benefits from keeping the wrist slightly dropped.

## What's Next

Position 3 marks the midpoint of the five-position cycle. Position 4 (next) moves higher into the upper neck and features a distinctive property: the b6 appears immediately adjacent to the 5th on both outer strings, giving descending phrases a particularly classical minor quality from the very first note.
