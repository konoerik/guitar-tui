## Position 2 — D-Shape (frets 7–10)

Position 2 begins at fret 7. The root moves to the D string (fret 7) and the B string (fret 10) — the D-shape position characteristic. This position has 16 notes across 6 strings, making it one of the denser positions in the scale.

## Where the b6 Lives Here

In Position 2, the b6 (F) appears on the A string (fret 8) and the G string (fret 10). The A string placement is notable: the sequence 7–8–10 on that string spells out 5–b6–b7 — three notes including the characteristic b6 — making the upper strings of this position rich with minor color.

## Connecting to Position 1

The overlap between Position 1 and Position 2 is at fret 7–8 on the low E string. The b3 (C) at fret 8 is the last note of Position 1 on string 6 and the second note of Position 2. Practice descending through Position 1 on strings 1–2, then slide back down into Position 2 via string 6 frets 10–8–7.

## Fingering

Index at fret 7, middle at 8, ring at 9, pinky at 10. The D string has notes at frets 7, 9, 10 — index, ring, pinky. The low E string has notes at 7, 8, 10 — index, middle, pinky (skipping ring). Keep the hand centered around fret 8–9 and stretch for the fret 10 notes with the pinky.

## What's Next

Position 2 is one of the richest positions in the scale. Position 3 (next) centers on the 12th-fret octave — the easiest root to find on the neck — and brings the b6 to the upper strings, where its characteristic minor color is most audible.
