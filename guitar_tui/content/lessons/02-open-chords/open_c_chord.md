## Finger Placement

C major is widely considered the most demanding open chord in this track. The difficulty is not the shape itself but the index finger: it must arch steeply at the first knuckle to fret the B string without touching the open high e string immediately above it.


- **Index finger** — 1st fret, B string
- **Middle finger** — 2nd fret, D string
- **Ring finger** — 3rd fret, A string
- Mute the low E — do not strum it

The open G string in the middle must ring freely with no finger touching it. Check that the middle finger is not drooping onto it.

## Common Mistakes

- **High e muted** — the index finger tip is resting against the high e string. This is the defining challenge of C major. Raise the index knuckle until the high e rings freely, then lock in that arch.
- **Open G muted** — the middle finger is leaning into the G string. Keep the middle finger tip pointing straight down.
- **A string buzz** — the ring finger at the 3rd fret needs to be right behind the fret wire. A long stretch for some hands; with repetition, the reach becomes natural.
- **Low E accidentally strummed** — start the strum from the A string. The low E produces a wrong note (E instead of C) that makes the chord sound off.
