## About This Chord

F major is the most physically demanding chord in this track. The index finger must barre all six strings at the 1st fret — the hardest position on the neck because the strings are furthest from the nut and require the most pressure. Many beginners take weeks or months to play F cleanly. That is normal.

## Barre Technique

The 1st fret full barre is the foundation. Lay your index finger flat across all six strings at the 1st fret and strum. Every string must ring. Common adjustments:

- **Roll the index finger slightly toward the nut** — the bony edge of the finger (facing toward the headstock) frets more cleanly than the soft pad.
- **Keep the thumb behind the neck, centred** — not over the top.
- **Press from the base knuckle, not the fingertip** — the entire length of the index must be active.

## Finger Placement

```diagram
type: chord
title: F Major
frets: [1, 3, 3, 2, 1, 1]
fingers: [1, 3, 4, 2, 1, 1]
barre:
  fret: 1
  from: 1
  to: 6
  finger: 1
```

- **Index finger** — full barre across all six strings at fret 1
- **Middle finger** — 2nd fret, G string
- **Ring finger** — 3rd fret, A string
- **Pinky finger** — 3rd fret, D string

The middle, ring, and pinky fingers form the same relative shape as an open E major chord — shifted up by one fret, with the index barre taking the place of the nut. This is the *E-shape barre chord* — the same principle will work for any major chord anywhere on the neck.

Build in layers:
1. Index barre (fret 1, all six strings)
2. Middle finger (G, fret 2)
3. Ring and pinky (A and D, fret 3)

The low E and high e (both fret 1) depend entirely on the barre. If either buzzes, return to practising the barre alone before adding the other fingers.

## Why the 1st Fret Is Hard

String tension is highest near the nut. The further up the neck you go, the easier barre chords become. Once F is clean, try the same E-shape barre at fret 3 (G major), fret 5 (A major), or fret 7 (B major). These are significantly easier and help build the technique you bring back to fret 1.

## Common Mistakes

- **Low E buzz** — the barre is not covering the outer strings firmly. Roll the index slightly toward the nut edge and increase pressure at the base knuckle.
- **B string dead** — the barre often collapses at the B string because that is where most fingers have a natural joint crease. Adjust the index finger position until the crease moves off the B string.
- **Hand fatigue** — this is the most physically demanding chord in the beginner set. Take breaks. Strength builds over days and weeks, not in a single session. Practise in short bursts rather than pushing through pain.
