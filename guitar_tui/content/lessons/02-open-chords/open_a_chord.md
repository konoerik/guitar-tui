## Finger Placement

A major places three fingers side by side on the 2nd fret of strings D, G, and B. The low E string is muted; the A and high e strings ring open. The tight spacing is the main challenge.

```diagram

title: A Major
frets: [null, 0, 2, 2, 2, 0]
fingers: [null, null, 1, 2, 3, null]
```

- **Index finger** — 2nd fret, D string
- **Middle finger** — 2nd fret, G string
- **Ring finger** — 2nd fret, B string

Three fingers must fit into a single fret without touching the open high e string above or the open A string below. Press the very tips of each finger and keep the knuckles arched.

**Alternative fingering**: some players use the ring finger barred flat across strings D, G, and B at the 2nd fret. This can be easier on smaller hands, and it frees the index finger for transitions. Experiment — both fingerings are widely used.

If the high e sounds muted or buzzy, the ring finger is leaning into it. Tilt the ring finger's tip back so it clears the string.

## Common Mistakes

- **High e muted by ring finger** — the most common issue. The ring finger tip must point directly downward, not tilt toward the high e string.
- **Fingers not close enough to fret wires** — with three fingers sharing one fret, there is a temptation to spread them. Keep each tip as close to its wire as possible.
- **Accidentally strumming the low E** — rest the heel of the strumming hand lightly against it, or practise angling the pick downward from the A string. The low E produces a dissonant note that muddies the chord.
