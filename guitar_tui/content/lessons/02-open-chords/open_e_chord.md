## Finger Placement

E major is one of the few open chords where all six strings ring. The two open low and high E strings reinforce the root on both ends of the strum, giving the chord its full, resonant quality.

```diagram
type: chord
title: E Major
frets: [0, 2, 2, 1, 0, 0]
fingers: [null, 2, 3, 1, null, null]
```

- **Index finger** — 1st fret, G string
- **Middle finger** — 2nd fret, A string
- **Ring finger** — 2nd fret, D string

Keep all three fingertips well arched. The index finger on the G string is the trickiest placement — it must not lean into the open B string directly beside it.

Work through this slowly. If the G string buzzes, the index finger is too close to the 2nd fret wire — pull it back toward the 1st fret wire.

## Common Mistakes

- **G string buzz** — the index finger tip is not arched enough, or is leaning backward off the 1st fret wire. Press the very tip of the finger, as close to the wire as possible.
- **B string muted** — the index finger is leaning slightly into the open B string. Raise the knuckle.
- **D string not ringing** — the ring finger is not pressing firmly at the 2nd fret. Check that all three fingers are close to their respective fret wires.
