## The Listening Question

Romani (gypsy) violin music has a quality that even harmonic minor cannot reach — a keening, doubled exoticism, as if the scale leaps twice. It does. Hungarian minor takes harmonic minor's augmented 2nd and adds a second one, by raising the 4th:

| Scale | Intervals | Notes (key of A) |
|-------|-----------|------------------|
| A Harmonic Minor | 1 2 b3 4 5 b6 7 | A B C D E F G# |
| A Hungarian Minor | 1 2 b3 **#4** 5 b6 7 | A B C **D#** E F G# |

Two augmented 2nds now frame the 5th: **b3 → #4** (C to D#) and **b6 → 7** (F to G#). Two of the scale's seven steps are three-fret leaps.

Compare with harmonic minor position 1 in the previous lesson: the only change is D → D# (string 5, fret 5 → 6; string 3, fret 7 → 8). One fret, and the scale doubles its drama. The Key View marks the #4 with ◆.

## The Sound

- The **#4** pulls upward into the 5th — a leading tone *inside* the scale, aimed at E instead of A
- The **7** pulls up into the root, as in harmonic minor
- Between them sits the 5th, approached from below on both sides — melodies orbit it

Chord-wise the scale resists neat stacking (the #4 breaks the usual triad ladder), so the tradition treats it melodically: a drone or a simple **i – V** (Am – E) underneath, with the scale supplying all the color on top.

## Where You Have Heard It

- **Romani violin and cimbalom music** — the czardas repertoire
- **Liszt and Brahms** — the "Hungarian" pieces borrow it directly
- **Film scores** — instant Old World atmosphere
- **Metal and neoclassical guitar** — the doubled aug-2nd run at speed

## Practice

1. Alternate harmonic minor and Hungarian minor in position 1. The single changed note (D → D#) recalibrates your ear each pass.
2. Isolate the two leaps: C → D# (string 6 fret 8 → string 5 fret 6) and F → G# (string 5 fret 8 → string 4 fret 6). Play leap–resolve: C–D#–E, F–G#–A.
3. Loop a bare **Am** (or Am – E) and improvise slowly. This scale rewards space — hold the #4 and let it lean.

**Hungarian Augmented Seconds** in [4] Practice threads both leaps in a single rising phrase.
