## The Listening Question

Think of the shimmer that plays in an old film when a dream sequence begins — rising, weightless, refusing to land. That is the whole tone scale, and its floating quality is not a mood; it is arithmetic.

Every scale you have learned gets its sense of direction from **half steps**. The major scale's 7 leans into the root; Phrygian's b2 leans down onto it. The whole tone scale has no half steps at all:

| Scale | Intervals | Notes (key of C) |
|-------|-----------|------------------|
| C Whole Tone | 1 2 3 #4 #5 b7 | C D E F# G# Bb |

Six notes, each a whole step from the next. No note is closer to any other; no note leads anywhere. The result: no tension, no resolution, no gravity. It floats because *nothing pulls*.

## Perfect Symmetry

The scale is **symmetric**: transpose it up a whole step and you get the same six notes. Consequences:

- There are really only **two** whole tone scales in existence — C's collection and Db's. Every other root reuses one of them.
- On the fretboard, the pattern repeats every 2 frets. Two positions cover the entire neck:

Slide this shape up 2 frets: same notes. Up 1 fret: the *other* whole tone scale. In the Key View, notice there is no diatonic chord strip — with no perfect 5th anywhere in the scale, ordinary triads cannot form.

## The One Chord It Loves

Stack alternating scale notes and you get augmented triads (1–3–#5) and, crucially, the **dominant 7♯5** chord. Jazz players reach for whole tone precisely when a 7#5 appears: over C7#5, every note of C whole tone is a chord tone or a natural color. It is the rare scale with no wrong notes over its chord — and no resting note either.

## Where You Have Heard It

- **Debussy** — *Voiles* is built almost entirely on it
- **Thelonious Monk** — his signature run up the scale, both hands, ending nowhere
- **Stevie Wonder** — the intro to "You Are the Sunshine of My Life"
- **Film and cartoons** — the universal cue for dreams, flashbacks, and wooziness

## Practice

1. Play position 1 ascending and keep going — slide up 2 frets and continue the same shape. The scale laddering up the neck *is* the classic whole tone gesture.
2. Loop **C7** and float the scale over it. Where the plain 5th (G) of the backing chord rubs against the scale's #5, lean in — that rub is the sound. If you can voice C7#5, better still.
3. End phrases deliberately on different scale tones. None resolves. Learning to *choose* an ending without gravity is the skill here.

**Floating Whole Tone Line** in [4] Practice rises through the shape and hangs on the b7 — unresolved by design.

## What's Next

The whole tone scale is one of two famous symmetric scales. The other divides the octave into alternating whole and half steps — eight notes of pure tension: the diminished scale.
