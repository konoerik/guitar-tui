## Position 5 — G-Shape (frets 4–8)

Position 5 fills the gap between Position 4 (ending at fret 5) and Position 1 (starting at fret 7). It spans frets 4–8, with the root C on the G string at fret 5 and the high e string at fret 8. This is the G-shape position — the widest-spanning of the five, with 17 notes across 6 strings.

## 17 Notes — The Dense Shape

Position 5 has more notes than any other major scale position in the CAGED system. Strings 6, 5, 3, and 1 all carry three notes each. This density makes Position 5 ideal for flowing scalar runs — there are no strings with only one note where momentum breaks.

## The Root at Three Points

The root C appears on string 6 (fret 8), string 3 (fret 5), and string 1 (fret 8). The outer strings again share the same fret for the root — fret 8 on both low and high E — which is a useful anchor when navigating the shape.

## The 4th on the B String

The B string in this position carries the 4th degree (F in C major) at fret 6 — between the 3rd at fret 5 and the 5th at fret 8. The 4th is the note that creates a half-step against the 3rd (E→F), the second natural semitone pair in the major scale. Playing F (fret 6) down to E (fret 5) on the B string while sustaining or bending is a characteristic sound of major scale melodic playing.

## Completing the Cycle

| Pos | CAGED | Fret range | Root strings |
|-----|-------|-----------|--------------|
| 1 | E | 7–10 | 6, 4, 1 |
| 2 | D | 9–13 | 4, 2 |
| 3 | C | 12–15 | 5, 2 |
| 4 | A | 1–5 | 5, 3, 2 |
| 5 | G | 4–8 | 6, 3, 1 |

Position 5 ends at fret 8 on string 6 — the same fret where Position 1 has its root. The cycle is complete; position 1 resumes from there.

## Fingering

The G string starts at fret 4 (index), which is one fret lower than the other strings. Keep the index available for fret 4 on string 3 without displacing the hand from its overall fret 5–8 position. The B string spans 5–6–8 (index, middle, pinky). The D string has only two notes (5, 7) and needs no third finger.
