## Position 4 — A-Shape (frets 1–5)

Position 4 of C major sits in the lowest register of the neck, frets 1–5. The root C appears on the A string at fret 3, the G string at fret 5, and unusually, the B string at fret 1 — this B-string root at fret 1 is the lowest C on the guitar in standard tuning. This is the A-shape position.

## Three Roots in One Position

Position 4 contains the highest density of root notes of any C major position — three C notes are directly accessible: A string fret 3, G string fret 5, and B string fret 1. This makes Position 4 particularly valuable for melodic playing that frequently returns to the tonic, and for building phrases that cycle through different octave versions of the root.

## The B String Root at Fret 1

The C at fret 1 on the B string is a standout feature. It is one semitone above the open B string — a natural half-step (B–C is a natural semitone pair). Playing open B then fret 1 (C) demonstrates the half-step resolution in the clearest physical way possible on the instrument.

## Connecting to Position 5

Position 5 (the G-shape, frets 4–8) begins right where Position 4 ends — the 5th degree (G) at fret 5 on the low E string is shared between both positions. The overlap is at the high end of Position 4 and the low end of Position 5.

## Fingering

The fret span is 1–5. The A string runs 2–3–5 (index, middle, pinky — note: starts at fret 2). The D string also starts at fret 2. The G string runs 2–4–5 (index, ring, pinky). The B string starts at fret 1 (index). The low E string starts at fret 3 (no low-end notes below fret 3 for this position).
