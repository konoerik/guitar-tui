## Position 3 — C-Shape (frets 12–15)

Position 3 occupies frets 12 to 15. The root C appears on the A string at fret 15 and the B string at fret 13. The 12th fret octave landmark anchors this position clearly — several important notes land exactly at fret 12.

## Starting on the 3rd

Position 3 begins on the 3rd degree (E in C major) at fret 12 on the low E string. The first two notes ascending on the low E string are 3rd–4th (E–F, frets 12–13) — a half-step, one of the two natural semitones in the major scale. This half-step at the start of the position is immediately recognisable to the ear and is useful for identifying where you are harmonically.

## Symmetry of Outer Strings

Strings 6 and 1 both carry three notes at frets 12, 13, 15. The outer strings are identical in fret pattern — a feature also present in Position 1 for the natural minor. This symmetry is a memory aid: the shape mirrors itself between the lowest and highest strings.

## Fingering

Fret span 12–15, four frets, one-finger-per-fret standard assignment. The A string runs 12–14–15 (index, ring, pinky — skipping middle). The D string also runs 12–14–15. The G string has only two notes (12, 14 — index, ring). The B string starts at fret 13 (middle) due to the B-string offset.
