## What Is a Bend?

A **bend** pushes or pulls a string sideways across the fretboard to raise its pitch. Fret the 8th fret of the B string and push it upward — the note rises. A **whole-step bend** raises the pitch by two semitones, reaching the same pitch as two frets higher.

In tab notation, `8b` means bend that note. `8b10` means bend until it sounds like fret 10 — an exact pitch target for training your ear.

## A Blues Bend-and-Release

The bend-and-release is the cornerstone of blues phrasing: push the note up to its target pitch, hold it there for a beat, then release it slowly back down.

Fret 8 on the B string is G — the b7 in the key of A. A whole-step bend raises it to A (the pitch of fret 10), which is the root of the A7 chord. The bend creates tension; arriving at the root resolves it.

## Half-Step vs. Whole-Step

Two bend widths appear constantly in blues and rock:

| Bend | Semitones | Sound |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Half-step | 1 | Questioning, unstable |
| Whole-step | 2 | Reaching, vocal — the blues standard |

Whole-step bends dominate blues phrasing. Half-step bends create a more ambiguous, searching sound, common in country and jazz-inflected playing.

## Hand Technique

Use your **ring finger** to fret the bending note. Place your **middle and index fingers** on the same string behind the ring finger — all three fingers push the string together. A single-finger bend is weak and unstable; the three-finger grip gives leverage and pitch control.

String direction by position:

- **B and G strings**: push upward (toward the ceiling)
- **Low E, A, D strings**: push downward
- **High e**: push downward to avoid slipping off the fretboard

## Checking Your Pitch

Play the target fret first to memorize its pitch. Then bend from the lower fret and match it by ear. A bend that falls short — landing between semitones — is the most common beginner mistake. You must push far enough to fully reach the target.

## Practice

1. Fret the B string at fret 10 and memorize the pitch (A)
2. Fret 8 and push the string up until you match that pitch exactly
3. Hold the bent note for two full beats before releasing
4. Release slowly and controlled — not a snap
