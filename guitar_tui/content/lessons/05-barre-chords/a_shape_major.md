## The Second Moveable Shape

The A-shape barre is derived from the open A chord. The index finger barres strings 1–5 (string 6 is muted), and the remaining fingers hold the A voicing above. The root sits on **string 5** (the A string), not string 6.

## Root Positions on String 5

The A string note at each fret determines which chord you are playing:

| Root on A string | Fret | Chord |
|----------------|------|-------|
| Bb | 1 | Bb major |
| B | 2 | B major |
| C | 3 | C major |
| C# | 4 | C# major |
| D | 5 | D major |
| Eb | 6 | Eb major |
| E | 7 | E major |
| F | 8 | F major |
| F# | 9 | F# major |
| G | 10 | G major |
| Ab | 11 | Ab major |

## Fingering Options

The three notes on strings 4, 3, 2 (all at two frets above the barre) present a fingering challenge because they cannot all be covered by individual fingers in the standard way. Two common approaches:

**Ring finger mini-barre** (shown above): Roll the ring finger flat to cover strings 4, 3, and 2 simultaneously at the same fret. This is the most common approach and is what gives the shape its slightly uneven sound (the mini-barre tends to be less precise than individual fingers).

**Individual fingers**: Place middle finger on string 4, ring on string 3, pinky on string 2 — all at two frets above the barre. Requires stretching but produces a clearer, more ringing tone.

Either approach is valid. The ring-barre is faster in progressions; the individual-finger approach is cleaner for arpeggios.

## The E-Shape vs. A-Shape

For any given chord, you can use either an E-shape or A-shape barre depending on position. For C major:
- **E-shape**: fret 8 on the low E string
- **A-shape**: fret 3 on the A string

Both produce C major, but the E-shape sits higher on the neck (brighter, thinner tone), while the A-shape at fret 3 sits lower (fuller, more open sound). Choosing between them is a tonal and positional decision — whichever fret puts you closest to where you need to be for the next chord change.

## Muting String 6

The low E string must be muted — it does not belong in the A-shape chord. Options:
- Let the side of the index finger touch string 6 lightly (it will mute without sounding)
- Avoid picking string 6 when strumming
- Use the thumb to lightly damp string 6 from behind the neck (common in country and rock playing)
