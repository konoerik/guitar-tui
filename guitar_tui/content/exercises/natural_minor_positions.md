## The Exercise

Every pair of adjacent natural minor positions shares a two-fret overlap zone
where the same notes appear in both shapes. Finding and playing through these
overlaps is how you stop thinking in boxes and start thinking in one continuous
scale.

Start in Position 1 (A natural minor, root at fret 5 low E). Ascend to the
top of Position 1, then continue into Position 2 without stopping — the
top notes of Position 1 are also the bottom notes of Position 2.

The C note appears in both positions — it is the seam. Once you can cross it
without hesitation, the two positions become one shape.

## Rules

- **60 BPM**, eighth notes. Slow enough to hear every note cleanly.
- Use alternate picking throughout (down-up-down-up).
- At the handoff point, do not pause — the transition should feel as smooth
  as moving between any two notes within a single position.
- Once comfortable ascending, practice descending: start in Position 2,
  cross back into Position 1.

## Progression

Work through all five handoffs in order:
1. Position 1 → Position 2 (this exercise)
2. Position 2 → Position 3
3. Position 3 → Position 4
4. Position 4 → Position 5
5. Position 5 → Position 1 (wraps around the octave)

Then practice non-adjacent jumps: play Position 1, jump directly to Position 3.
This builds the fretboard geography awareness that makes position choice in
improvisation feel instinctive rather than calculated.
