## The Exercise

Form any open chord shape. Instead of strumming, pluck each string individually
from low E to high e, listening carefully to each note. Every string that should
ring must ring cleanly; every string that should be muted must produce no sound.

Repeat for every open chord shape you know. Run through the full set:
E · Em · A · Am · D · Dm · G · C.

## What to listen for

**Buzzing** — a finger is not pressing close enough to the fret, or an adjacent
finger is accidentally touching the string.

**Dead notes** — a finger is muting an adjacent string it should not be
touching. Check your finger arch: knuckles should be raised so only the
fingertip contacts the fretboard.

**Weak or thin tone** — the fretting pressure is insufficient. Press until the
buzz disappears, then notice how much pressure that actually required — usually
less than expected.

## Rules

- No metronome. This is a tone exercise, not a timing exercise.
- Hold the full chord shape while arpegggiating — do not release and reform
  between strings.
- Identify the specific string that buzzes or mutes before moving on. Fix it,
  then re-arpeggiate the full chord from the beginning.

## Progression

Once every string rings cleanly in a static hold:
1. Strum the chord, then immediately arpeggiate — confirm the strum did not
   shift any finger position.
2. Transition between two chords (e.g. G → D) and arpeggiate each after
   landing — confirm the transition does not introduce any buzz.
3. Apply to every new chord shape you learn before moving to strumming it.
