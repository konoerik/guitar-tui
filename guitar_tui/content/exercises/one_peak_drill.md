## The Exercise

Over a looped **Am** vamp, improvise short phrases (one or two bars) in A minor pentatonic position 1. Constraint: each phrase has **one peak note, played once**. Before each phrase, name the peak aloud — "peak on G" — then build a line that rises to it and comes away from it without touching it again.

## Rules

- Declare the peak **before** playing. Discovering it mid-phrase is the noodling this drill exists to cure.
- The peak is touched once. A second visit disqualifies the phrase — start a new one.
- Hold the peak longer than its neighbors; arrival deserves time.
- Land somewhere chosen: root for a full stop, 5th to stay open. Say the landing too, once the peak habit is easy.

## Progression

1. Ten consecutive legal phrases, peaks anywhere in position 1.
2. Ladder the peaks: each phrase's peak one scale-step higher than the last, until the position runs out — a solo's rising arc in miniature.
3. Mix shapes: alternate arc phrases (peak in the middle) with descent phrases (peak first note).
4. Advanced: same drill in position 2, then across the position 1–2 boundary.
