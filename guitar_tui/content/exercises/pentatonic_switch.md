## The Exercise

Loop a slow **blues in A** (a single A7 vamp works). Improvise in strict
alternation: one phrase from A **major** pentatonic (frets 2–5), rest, one
phrase from A **minor** pentatonic (frets 5–8), rest, repeat. The rest
between phrases is mandatory — it is where the hand relocates and the ear
registers the color change.

## Rules

- Strict alternation — no two consecutive phrases in the same color, even
  when one is going well. The discipline is the drill.
- Phrases stay short: one or two bars. Long phrases hide a lazy switch.
- Every phrase ends on a held note. If you cannot say which scale the phrase
  used, it does not count.
- Do not skip the rests. A switch without a breath is how the two scales
  smear into one six-note blur.

## Progression

1. The warm-up cell above, alternating, until fluent at 90 BPM.
2. Free phrases, strict alternation, single A7 vamp. Ten clean pairs.
3. Full 12-bar form: major pentatonic over the I chord (A7), minor over the
   IV (D7), either over the V (E7). The switch now follows the form instead
   of the phrase count.
4. Advanced: switch *inside* a phrase at a rest or a held note — the B.B.
   King move proper. Record a chorus and check the switches are audible.
