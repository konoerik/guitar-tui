## The Exercise

Eighth-note downstrokes on E5, alternating texture every two beats: four muted chugs, four open rings, repeat. The rhythm never changes — only the palm state does.

## Rules

- **70–90 BPM.** The switch happens *between* strokes — stroke 4 is fully muted, stroke 5 fully open, nothing smeared in between.
- The palm re-seats by feel, not by looking. Eyes on the fretting hand or nowhere.
- Muted strokes must keep their pitch (a low, tight E, not a dead thud) — if pitch disappears, the palm has crept too far from the bridge.
- Consistency check: each group of four should sound like one stroke repeated, same attack and volume.

## Progression

1. Two-beat alternation as written, until ten cycles pass with no scrape or gap at the switch.
2. Shrink the window: alternate every beat (two muted, two open), then every stroke (muted-open-muted-open).
3. Move the figure: E5 → G5 (frets 3/5) → A5 (frets 5/7), keeping the mute anchored through the shifts.
4. Advanced: hold a *half-muted* middle state for a bar between the extremes — the palm as a continuous tone control.
