## The Exercise

Unlike the chromatic warm-up (which stays on one string), the spider crosses
strings with each finger. The pattern: finger 1 on string 6, finger 2 on string
5, finger 3 on string 4, finger 4 on string 3 — then shift one string down and
repeat.

The pattern shifts one string per group of four notes, climbing diagonally
across the neck.

## Rules

- **60 BPM**, sixteenth notes. Each click = one note.
- Fret finger 1 at fret 1, finger 2 at fret 2, finger 3 at fret 3, finger 4 at fret 4.
- Every fretting finger holds its note until the picking hand has moved to the next string.
- Do not mute adjacent strings by accident — arch your fingers.

## Why It Works

The chromatic warm-up builds single-string strength. The spider builds
*cross-string coordination* — your picking hand must track the fretting hand
across strings, and your fretting fingers must stay placed without muting
neighboring strings. This is the primary coordination challenge in all
scale playing.
