## The Exercise

Loop **Am – E**, one bar each. Before each bar arrives, say the target aloud —
a note belonging to the coming chord ("A" for Am, "G#" for E) — then build a
short phrase that lands that note **exactly on the chord's downbeat**. The
phrase before the downbeat is free; the downbeat itself is not.

## Rules

- The target is named **before** the bar, out loud. Finding it as you land is
  a different (easier) skill and not this drill.
- The target lands on beat one — not beat one-and-a-half. Early or late, the
  bar fails; start the next one.
- Between downbeats anything from A natural minor or the pentatonic box is
  legal. The freedom between targets is the point: constrained downbeats,
  free everything else.
- A missed reach still counts if the *right* note was named — the naming is
  half the drill.

## Progression

1. Am – E loop, targets limited to roots (A, then E). Ten clean bars.
2. Same loop, targets from anywhere in each chord: Am = A/C/E, E = E/G#/B.
   The G# target is the one to drill hardest — it is the only note outside
   the key's scale.
3. Full folk changes: **Am – C – D – F – Am – E – Am**, one target per bar.
   The D bar's F# and the F bar's F natural, one after the other, are the
   final exam within the drill.
4. Advanced: two targets per bar — beat one and beat three — turning the
   drill into the capstone lesson's target line, improvised live.
