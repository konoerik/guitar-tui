## The Exercise

Over a metronome (or a looped drum groove), strum an A minor chord **only on the "&" of every beat** — upstrokes — for as long as you can keep it locked. Every number (1, 2, 3, 4) is a silent down-pass.

## Rules

- **65–75 BPM.** Slower is harder here — more silence to stay honest through.
- The down-pass on each number must physically happen. If the hand hovers waiting for the "&", the drill is defeated and your timing will drift within two bars.
- Count aloud with the numbers *emphasized* ("**1** and **2** and") even though you play the opposite — the voice holds the grid your hands are avoiding.
- Watch for the classic failure: after 8–16 bars the upstrokes migrate onto the numbers without you noticing. Record a minute and check.

## Progression

1. Sixteen clean bars on Am at 70 BPM.
2. Add changes: Am–Dm–E–Am, two bars each, changing on a silent beat 1.
3. Alternate bars: one bar of downbeat-only strums, one bar of off-beats only. The switch is the test.
4. Advanced: move to the sixteenth grid — strum only the "e" and "a" slots (all upstrokes), numbers and "&"s silent.
