## The Exercise

Play the open low E string with strict alternating pick strokes: down, up,
down, up — continuous and even. Then fret individual notes and apply the
same strict alternation.

## Rules

- **60 BPM**, sixteenth notes. The downstroke lands on the click; the upstroke
  is exactly halfway between clicks.
- The pick travels the same distance on down and up strokes — equal motion,
  equal volume.
- Anchor the picking hand lightly on the bridge for stability.
- Do not look at the pick while playing. Look at the fretting hand.

## Progression

Once the open string is automatic, apply to:
1. A single fretted note alternating (as shown above, E5–E7)
2. A two-note pattern on adjacent strings (cross-picking)
3. Any scale pattern — the rule is always strict down-up regardless of string changes

## Common Mistakes

**Economy picking by accident** — letting the pick direction follow convenience
rather than strict alternation. Economy picking is a valid advanced technique,
but alternate picking must be mastered first.

**Uneven strokes** — downstrokes louder than upstrokes. Record yourself and
listen back. The two strokes should be indistinguishable.
