## The Exercise

Loop **Am – G – F – E** (one bar each). Improvise a one-bar question phrase over Am. Rest through G while its rhythm replays in your head. Then answer over F–E: **different pitches, the exact same rhythm**, ending on the root. The rhythm is the echo; the notes are the reply.

## Rules

- The answer's rhythm is a **clone** — same slots, same durations, same rest placement. Close is not enough; the ear only hears the pairing when it is exact.
- Question ends off the root (5th, b7, or 2nd); answer ends on it.
- One full bar of genuine rest between the two. Use it to hold the rhythm, not to plan pitches — the pitches should be found in the moment, on the rails of the rhythm.
- Keep questions simple at first: four to six notes. Complex rhythms cannot be cloned until plain ones can.

## Progression

1. Ten pairs where the answer's rhythm survives intact.
2. Swap roles: pre-compose the *answer*, improvise questions that set it up.
3. Inversion pairs: answer clones the rhythm **and** mirrors the contour (question rises, answer falls).
4. Advanced: two-bar questions with two-bar answers — the same discipline at solo-section scale.
