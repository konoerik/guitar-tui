## The Exercise

A position shift is when the entire fretting hand moves along the neck to a new home fret. Without smooth shifts, scale positions remain isolated boxes. This exercise builds the muscle memory for one of the most important shifts in pentatonic playing: moving from position 1 (hand centered around fret 5) to position 2 (hand centered around fret 8).

The key insight: the same fret (8) on the same strings plays different fingers in each position. In position 1, fret 8 is the **pinky**. After the shift, fret 8 is the **index**. That is the shift.

Measure 1 is position 1: index finger at fret 5, pinky reaches fret 8. Measure 2 is position 2: index finger at fret 8, ring finger reaches fret 10. The shift happens in the silence between measures — the hand slides up the neck so that fret 8 is now under the index finger.

## Rules

- **60 BPM**, four beats per measure. Leave a deliberate gap between the two measures while learning — that gap is the shift.
- The shift should be a **smooth slide**, not a jump. The index finger (or whichever finger anchors the new position) should glide along the string to the target fret rather than lifting and landing.
- The first note of measure 2 must land **on the beat**, not before or after. The shift is complete when the index is in place; the note rings the moment the beat arrives.
- As tempo increases, the gap disappears. The shift becomes part of the phrase rather than a pause.

## Pivot Note Approach

A useful technique: let the last note of position 1 (fret 8 on the high e string) ring while the hand shifts. The note continues sounding as the index slides up to fret 8. When the index arrives, you are already in position 2 — the ring and pinky are free to reach frets 10 and beyond.

## Progression

1. Master this shift (positions 1 → 2) in both directions
2. Shift from position 2 → position 3 (hand moves from fret 8 to fret 10-12 area)
3. Practice long shifts: jump from position 1 to position 4 or 5 in one move
4. Apply shifts within phrases: play 2 notes in position 1, shift mid-phrase, land in position 3, and continue without breaking musical continuity
5. Add the shift to the position-linking exercise (Natural Minor Position Connector) for full-scale connectivity
