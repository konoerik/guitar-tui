## The Exercise

The half-bar pattern **D · · U · U D ·** (strums on 1, the "a" of 1, the "e" of 2, and the "&" of 2), played twice per bar, through **Am – F – C – G**, one bar each. The strumming hand moves in unbroken sixteenths throughout; chord changes land exactly on each bar's beat 1.

## Rules

- **60–70 BPM** to start. Count "1 e & a 2 e & a" aloud — non-negotiable for the first week.
- The hand never stops: misses are full passes that skim above the strings.
- **Change chords on time even if the shape is incomplete.** Rhythm outranks finger perfection in this drill; the fingers catch up.
- Fret-hand fingers begin moving to the next chord during the final miss slot of each bar.

## Progression

1. One chord (Am) until the pattern runs without counting errors.
2. Two chords (Am–F), then the full four-bar cycle.
3. Raise tempo in steps of 5 BPM; stop raising when the pattern's misses start landing as accidental hits.
4. Advanced: same progression, but displace the pattern to start on beat 3 of each bar — the second half leads.
