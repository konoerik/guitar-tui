## The Exercise

Set the metronome to 70 BPM. On a single E5 chord, strum one bar of each subdivision in sequence — up the pyramid and back down — without stopping and without the click drifting:

quarters → eighths → sixteenths → eighths → quarters

## Rules

- **70 BPM**, click on every quarter note. Count each bar's subdivision aloud ("1 2 3 4" / "1 & 2 &" / "1 e & a").
- The transition happens **at the bar line, without hesitation** — the first strum of the new bar lands exactly on beat 1.
- Strict alternation on eighths and sixteenths: on eighths, D on the numbers and U on the "&"s; on sixteenths, D on the numbers and "&"s, U on the "e"s and "a"s.
- If any transition rushes (the classic fault: sixteenths speeding up), drop 10 BPM and rebuild.

## Progression

1. Clean pyramid at 70 BPM for five consecutive cycles.
2. Raise to 80, then 90.
3. Swap the E5 for a full Am chord, then for a changing pair (Am–G, one pyramid each).
4. Advanced: two-beat rungs instead of full bars — the transitions come twice as fast.
