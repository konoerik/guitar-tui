## The Exercise

Loop four bars of **Am** (or Am–G–F–E, one bar each). Improvise freely from A minor pentatonic position 1 during bars 1–2; play *nothing* during bars 3–4. Repeat for the whole practice block. The rule is absolute: however good the idea that arrives in bar 3, it waits.

## Rules

- The rest bars are **counted** ("1 & 2 & …"), not waited out. You are still playing — the instrument is silence.
- End bar 2 deliberately: a held note released on purpose, not a phrase running out of road.
- During the rest, plan the next phrase — one decision minimum (its shape, its entry slot, or its landing note).
- No "just one note" leaks into bars 3–4. The itch to fill is the muscle being trained.

## Progression

1. Two-and-two as written for a full practice block (10 minutes).
2. Tighten to one-and-one: one bar playing, one bar silent — the decisions come twice as fast.
3. Loosen to three-and-one, which approximates real solo pacing, and keep the rest bar honest.
4. Advanced: two-and-two where each new phrase must *develop* the previous one (see the motif lessons) — restraint plus memory.
